Why This Is Bigger Than A Drug Launch

India has 89.8 million diabetics. Most never touched semaglutide — until now.

For five years, Ozempic and Wegovy existed in India the way luxury cars do — technically available, practically out of reach. The drug that rewired global obesity medicine was priced for a tiny urban elite.

That changes this week. The GLP-1 market — already worth ₹1,446 crore and growing at a pace that shocked even optimists — just lost its price ceiling. CareEdge Ratings projects it hits ₹4,500–5,000 crore by 2030. That's a 3-4x expansion driven entirely by access.

With 254 million adults living with generalised obesity, India doesn't just have a diabetes problem. It has a metabolic crisis. And the pharma industry just dropped the most powerful weapon against it from ₹16,000 to ₹1,290 overnight.

The Defender's Gambit

Novo Nordisk didn't go quietly.

The day before India's semaglutide patent expired, the US FDA approved Wegovy HD — a 7.2mg dose delivering 20.7% mean weight loss. No Indian generic can match it yet. That's not coincidence. That's strategy.

💊
Wegovy HD Approval
7.2mg vs 2.4mg standard. 20.7% weight loss in STEP UP trial. Generics cannot replicate immediately. US launch April 2026.
📉
37% Price Cut (Nov 2025)
Pre-emptively slashed Indian Wegovy pricing months before patent expiry to reduce the generic price advantage gap.
🤝
Emcure Partnership
Partnered with Indian pharma Emcure to launch a second branded semaglutide. Fighting for the Indian market with a local face.
💊
Oral Wegovy Already in US
Once-daily oral semaglutide launched in the US in 2026. Removes the self-injection barrier — a massive patient segment.
